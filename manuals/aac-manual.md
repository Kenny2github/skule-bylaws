# For Candidates

## Role Summary
All applicants are accepted as this is a purely voluntary commitment. It's up to you how much you would like to engage in improving SKULEâ„¢'s academic quality of life. Once you have signed up, you will receive email invitations to, and when2meets for the subcommittees you signed up for + full AAC meetings (5x/year). Detailed subcommittee descriptions are in the section after basic information.

## General Responsibilities
1. The Proactive Subcommittee
   1. Propose/build initiatives to provide academic support/opportunities to students. 
   1. Create surveys to gather data/student opinions to support EngSoc student representatives in their advocacy.   
   1. Additional administrative tasks to keep existing EngSoc Academic supports updated (ex. Website review). 
1. The Reactive Subcommittee
   1. A monthly forum for students to share course/program concerns, seek advice and coordinate responses. Class Representatives and Discipline Club Academic Directors are members by default. 

## Required Qualifications
1. No experience/qualifications are required! Only an interest in creating change!

## Useful Qualifications
1. Academic Advocacy Experience
2. Class Representative Experience

## Role Takeaways
1. Transferable Skills: Develop Leadership / Advocacy / Written Communication Skills.
1. Technical: Develop Report Writing, Data Analysis, Survey Making Skills.
1. Understand behind-the-scenes Faculty-Student Politics / Relationships and how they shape your undergraduate degree. 

# Summary and Structure

## Role Summary
1. The Academic Advocacy Committee (AAC) is a group of students led by the EngSoc VP Academic that works with the Faculty to improve the academic experience of all engineering students. The AAC is organized into:
   1. The Proactive Subcommittee
   1. The Reactive Subcommittee
1. Students can join either or both the Proactive or Reactive subcommittees.

## Transition
1. The Academic Adovcacy Committee shall transition their successor as described in the [Transition Policy](../policies/transition-policy.md).

## Reporting Structure
1. All Proactive and Reactive Subcommittee members report directly to the [Vice President Academic](vpa-manual.md).
1. Executive positions may be created to spearhead different initiatives at the discretion of the VP Academic. Otherwise, the VP Academic assumes all responsibilities outlined  below. 

# Manual of Responsibilities

## Ongoing Responsibilities
1. VP Academic: Booking meeting spaces and time. 
1. Executive Members (Otherwise VP Academic if none appointed): 
   1. Moderating discussion for each topic. 
   1. Receiving progress updates and assigning action items (and associated timeframes for completion) for each topic. 
   1. Asynchronous check-ins / support for AAC members on the completion of action items from meetings.
1. General Members:
   1. Gathering information/opinions from students to identify gaps/concerns that must be addressed. 

## Time-fixed Responsibilities
1. General Members:
   1. Assigned action items within their associated timeframes, decided by the VP Academic or any designated executive.  
   1. Attending monthly meetings.
