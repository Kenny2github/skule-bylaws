# For Candidates

## Role Summary
The Hard Hat Café Managers are responsible for maintaining and operating the Hard Hat Café. They will oversee Cashiers and Executives who will help facilitate the operation of the cafe. 

## General Responsibilities
1. Ordering and managing stock
2. Managing staff
3. Managing finances
4. Managing daily operations

## Required Qualifications
1. Complete Cashier Training
2. Have a Food Handlers Certificate

## Useful Qualifications
1. Hard Hat Cafe Cashier
2. Experience managing budgets
3. Inventory tracking/management experience

## Role Takeaways
1. A Hard Hat Café Manager's missing qualifications listed in section 0.3 may be gained by exercising the role.

# Summary and Structure

## Role Summary
The Hard Hat Café Managers are responsible for maintaining and operating the Hard Hat Café. They will oversee Cashiers and Executives who will help facilitate the operation of the cafe. 

## Transition
1. The Hard Hat Café Managers shall transition their successor as described in the [Transition Policy](../policies/transition-policy.md).
1. The outgoing managers shall train the new managers in the Cafe
2. The outgoing managers shall create a master document consisting of:
   1. All vendor information
   2. Passwords
   3. Tasks
1. The new managers should catch up with Engineering Society Entities, such as the Vice President Finance and the Business Manager
2. Supplier contacts should be updated/renewed 

## Executive Structure
1. Vending Machine Manager
   1. Propose new items
   2. Ensure vending machines are stocked
   3. Remove any expired items
1. Assistant Manager
   1. Support the manager in their regular tasks
   2. Help with additional orders
   3. Promotional planning
   4. Event Planning
   5. Collect deliveries
1. Graphics
   1. Manage branding and Instagram account
   2. Organize logos
   3. Ensuring consistent branding of Hard Hat Cafe
   4. Can be a social media manager at the discretion of the managers
1. Finance Executive
   1. Take summaries from the POS machine and send them to the Engineering Society Business Manager
   2. Create meal deals and other promotional items
   3. Ensure a good profit margin

## Reporting Structure
1. The Hard Hat Café Managers are overseen by the [Vice President Finance](vpf-manual.md).

# Manual of Responsibilities

## Ordering and Managing Stock
1. Track expiring inventory
2. Coordinate and maintain communication with suppliers, most common being Costco and Core-Mark
3. Place an order at least once a month
4. Organize deliveries with vendors

## Operational Responsibilities
1. The executive team should be hired by mid-summer
2. Cashiers must be hired before the start of F!rosh Week
3. Must create a budget before the beginning of the school year
4. Managers need to open and close every day. If no managers are available, an EngSoc officer can open/close
5. Cash counting every day, after closing
6. Management of fresh foods
7. Updating the POS, managing pricing
8. Submit monthly financial reports (can be delegated)
9. Ensure proper licensing
   8. Cashiers need to be trained
   9. Ensure proper food practices
1. Manage vending machines
   1. Keep stocked and track expiration dates
   2. Ensure maintenance of the machines
1. Organize staff socials and other fun events
2. Deposit cash into the bank while still ensuring enough float is kept in the register

## Training Cashiers
1. Online training for cashiers must be completed before in-person training
1. In-person training consists of:
   1. Operation of the cafe
   2. How to use POS
   3. Run through of Operations
1. Cashiers must have a food handler's certificate to work

## Managing Cashiers
1. Gather schedules of all cashiers
2. Schedule 1-hour shifts, 1 or 2 times per week
3. Have a group chat so that people can cover each other's shifts
   4. If no one can cover a shift, the manager should cover the shift

## Cashier Responsibilities
1. Check if items are out of stock, and report to the managers if they are
2. Keep the cleanliness of the cafe
3. Ensure food quality and adhere to food safety standards
4. Keep pizza in the warmers
5. Warm up hot food in the air frier 

## Summer Responsibilities
1. Early May to mid June
   1. Start work on the budget after transitioning
   2. Create F!rosh Handbook blurb and contribute to the F!rosh Kits
   3. Introduce yourselves to suppliers
   4. Take inventory
   5. Make any necessary large purchases
   6. Set up Costco membership and Core-mark credit application
   7. Plan hiring of executives and cashiers
   8. Create goals for advertising
   9. Ensure Cafe licensing
1. Mid June to early July
   1. Conduct interviews for cashiers
   2. Select staff
   3. Contact vendors regarding pricing and deals they offer
1. August
   1. Conduct staff training
   2. Ensure all cashiers have their certificates
   3. Make timetables
   4. Begin placing orders for food, ensuring they arrive before the first day of operation
   5. Run in-person training
   6. Enter all items in the POS
   7. Tell UofT Food Services the opening date
