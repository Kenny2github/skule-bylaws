# Policy
## General
1. Purpose: To ensure consistent imagery for all of the Engineering Society's documents and presentations.

## The Engineering Society Logo
1. The Engineering Society logo must be used in all matters of governance and official business documents including (but not limited to) letterheads, sponsorship documents, and stationary used in communication with the University, the Faculty, and other external bodies.
1. Affiliated clubs and organizations may only display the Engineering Society logo in
promotional materials if:
   1. The club or organization receives sponsorship from the Engineering Society;
   1. Usage of the logo is limited to displaying the Engineering Society as a sponsor; and
   1. The promotional material does not give the indication that the club or organization is a part of the Engineering Society and does not create the impression that an event or service is run by the Engineering Society unless such is the case.
1. The Engineering Society reserves the right to prohibit clubs and organizations from using the logo.

## The Skule™ Logo
1. The Skule™ logo is to be used for the promotion of student life in the University of Toronto undergraduate engineering community and events and services that support it.
1. The use of the Skule™ trademark (either in name or image) is a privilege reserved solely for affiliated clubs. Unaffiliated clubs/groups may not use the Skule™ trademark in any manner, including, but not limited to, the club name, products, and promotional materials. No advertisements may claim to represent Skule™ as a whole nor indicate that the group is endorsed by the Engineering Society, unless told otherwise by the Vice-President Communications.
1. The term “Skule” is a trademark owned by the Engineering Society. Thus, all uses of the term must be followed by the trademark symbol (™).

## Other Graphics
1. All official business documents and promotional material pertaining to the Engineering Society, including (but not limited to) letterheads, sponsorship documents, and stationary used in Communication with the University, the Faculty, and other external bodies must adhere to the colour palette provided (see section 3.0).
1. All official business documents and promotional material pertaining to the Engineering Society, including (but not limited to) letterheads, sponsorship documents, and stationary used in Communication with the University, the Faculty, and other external bodies must adhere to the font guidelines provided (see section 3.1).

## Documents
1. All official business documents issued by the Engineering Society, both internal and external, must use their designated Masthead.
   1. All internal documentation must use the internal document masthead (see section 4.0.1).
   1. All external documentation must use the external document masthead (see section 4.0.2).
   1. All governing documents must use the governing document masthead (see section 4.0.3).

## Fonts
1. For a serif font, the Caecilia font should be used. ([Download Link](https://profont.net/family/caecilia-lt-std.html))
   1. Alternatively, Centaur is preferred, keeping in line with the official University of Toronto font guidelines. As Centaur is a licensed font, it must be purchased from a font foundry.
   1. An acceptable substitute font for Centaur and Caecilia is Times New Roman, as per the official University of Toronto font guidelines.
1. For a sans serif font, Gill Sans is preferred; it is a default font on most computers.
   1. Alternatively, the Lato font is acceptable. ([Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Lato))

## Style Guide
1. Document Layout:
   1. In a document with multiple sections (for example, a document with a main body and an appendix), all sections must be separated by a page break.
   1. All sections of the document (with the exception of the main body section) must be titled in bolded capital letters at the beginning of the section. The title should be the same size as the body text, and should be indented.
1. Document Numbering:
   1. In any given section, each subsection should be given a numerical value, starting at 1. It is also appropriate to begin at 0 if the information in the subsection contains information relevant to the rest of the subsections (such as a general subsection).
   1. Subsubsections should be assigned a lowercase letter of the alphabet, starting at a. Subsections within these subsections should be assigned numerical values in Roman numeral form.
   1. Figures referred to in the document are to be numbered as a subsection of whatever subsection they are included in, and are to be assigned a numerical value starting at 1. Figures should be labeled with bolded text centered below the figure.
   1. Appendices are to be given a capital letter of the alphabet (i.e. ISO Basic Latin Alphabet), starting at A.
