# General

## Purpose
1. This document outlines the journalistic practices to be followed by the publications of the Engineering Society, to ensure that the Society upholds its responsibilities as a publisher.

## Scope
1. This policy applies to all Engineering Society’s publications, namely the F!rosh Handbook, the Skule™ Agenda, the Skulebook, The Cannon, and The Toike Oike.

## Responsibilities
1. It is the responsibility of the VP Communications to enforce this policy for all Engineering Society Publications. The Editors of the aforementioned publications should uphold the standards in this document to the best of their ability.

## Standards
1. Journalistic practices must be followed with accordance to [Canadian Association of Journalists ethics guidelines](https://caj.ca/wp-content/uploads/Ethics-Guidelines-v2023.pdf) ([Web Archive](https://web.archive.org/web/20260423145224/https://caj.ca/wp-content/uploads/Ethics-Guidelines-v2023.pdf)).
1. Content depicting crime and illegal activity should follow the BBC’s Editorial Guidelines [Section 8: Reporting Crime and Anti-Social Behaviour](https://www.bbc.co.uk/editorialguidelines/guidelines/crime-and-anti-social-behaviour) ([Web Archive](https://web.archive.org/web/20260504234750/https://www.bbc.co.uk/editorialguidelines/guidelines/crime-and-anti-social-behaviour)). In using these guidelines, any reference to the “Director of Editorial Policy and Standards”, “Programme Legal Advice”, “Senior Editorial Figure”, “Commissioning Editor”, and “CBBC Interactive Executive Management Team” shall refer to the VP Communications.
1. Journalistic practices must be followed with accordance to the International Federation of Journalists’ [Global Charter of Ethics for Journalists](https://www.ifj.org/who/rules-and-policy/global-charter-of-ethics-for-journalists) ([Web Archive](https://web.archive.org/web/20260611140630/https://www.ifj.org/who/rules-and-policy/global-charter-of-ethics-for-journalists)), especially with regards to the article on discrimination. Note that the grounds for discrimination include but are not limited to the grounds protected under the Canadian Human Rights Act, namely: race, national or ethnic origin, colour, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, marital status, family status, disability, genetic characteristics, and any conviction for which a pardon has been granted or a record suspended.
1. Any content regarding mental illness or the depiction of mental illness shall follow the [Canadian Psychiatric Association’s Media Guidelines for Reporting on Suicide](https://www.cpa-apc.org/wp-content/uploads/Media-Guidelines-Suicide-Reporting-EN-2018.pdf) ([Web Archive](https://web.archive.org/web/20260312150229/https://www.cpa-apc.org/wp-content/uploads/Media-Guidelines-Suicide-Reporting-EN-2018.pdf)).
